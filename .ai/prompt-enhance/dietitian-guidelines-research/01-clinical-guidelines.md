# Clinical Guidelines & Frameworks Research

## Overview

This document provides comprehensive research on clinical guidelines and frameworks referenced in dietitian practice, focusing on European and global authoritative sources for clinical nutrition and medical nutrition therapy (MNT).

---

## 1. EFSA — Dietary Reference Values (DRVs) & Food-Based Dietary Guidelines

### Resource Status
- **URL**: https://www.efsa.europa.eu/en/topics/topic/nutrition
- **Status**: ✅ Active and accessible
- **Authority**: European Food Safety Authority (EFSA)
- **Last Verified**: 2025

### Key Information

**Purpose**: EFSA provides authoritative EU reference standards for nutrient targets, including percentage of energy intake (%EI) ranges, Population Reference Intakes (PRI), Adequate Intakes (AI), and Average Requirements (AR).

**Key Recommendations**:

1. **Macronutrient Distribution (%EI - Percentage of Energy Intake)**:
   - **Protein**: 10-35% of total energy intake
   - **Fat**: 20-35% of total energy intake
   - **Carbohydrates**: 45-65% of total energy intake

2. **Protein Requirements**:
   - General adult population: 0.83 g/kg body weight/day (PRI)
   - Higher requirements for specific populations (athletes, elderly, clinical conditions)

3. **Fat Requirements**:
   - Saturated fatty acids: <10% of total energy
   - Trans fatty acids: as low as possible
   - Essential fatty acids (linoleic acid, α-linolenic acid): specific AI values

4. **Carbohydrate Requirements**:
   - Total carbohydrates: 45-60% of total energy
   - Free sugars: <10% of total energy (WHO recommendation, referenced by EFSA)
   - Dietary fiber: 25 g/day for adults (AI)

### Access Methods
- **Web Access**: Direct access via EFSA website
- **PDF Summaries**: Available for download from EFSA nutrition topic pages
- **API Access**: Not available (web-based only)
- **Format**: HTML pages, PDF documents

### Citations
- EFSA Panel on Dietetic Products, Nutrition and Allergies (NDA). (2010). Scientific Opinion on Dietary Reference Values for carbohydrates and dietary fibre. EFSA Journal, 8(3), 1462.
- EFSA Panel on Dietetic Products, Nutrition and Allergies (NDA). (2012). Scientific Opinion on Dietary Reference Values for protein. EFSA Journal, 10(2), 2557.

## 2. ESPEN (European Society for Clinical Nutrition and Metabolism)

### Resource Status
- **URL**: https://www.espen.org/guidelines-home/espen-guidelines
- **Status**: ✅ Active and accessible
- **Authority**: European Society for Clinical Nutrition and Metabolism
- **Last Verified**: 2025

### Key Information

**Purpose**: ESPEN provides clinical nutrition guidelines for energy/protein prescription, adult & pediatric MNT, inpatient and community care.

**Key Guidelines**:

1. **Energy Requirements**:
   - Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) calculation using predictive equations
   - Physical Activity Level (PAL) multipliers
   - Illness stress factors for clinical conditions

2. **Protein Requirements**:
   - Healthy adults: 0.8-1.0 g/kg body weight/day
   - Clinical conditions: 1.2-2.0 g/kg body weight/day (depending on condition)
   - Critical illness: 1.2-1.5 g/kg body weight/day
   - Chronic kidney disease (pre-dialysis): 0.8-1.0 g/kg body weight/day

3. **Illness Stress Factors**:
   - Minor surgery: 1.0-1.2
   - Major surgery: 1.2-1.4
   - Sepsis: 1.4-1.6
   - Burns: 1.5-2.0
   - Trauma: 1.2-1.4

4. **Nutrition Support Indications**:
   - Malnutrition screening and assessment tools
   - Enteral vs. parenteral nutrition decision trees
   - Monitoring and reassessment protocols

### Access Methods
- **Web Access**: Direct access via ESPEN website
- **PDF Downloads**: Guidelines available as PDF documents
- **API Access**: Not available
- **Format**: PDF documents, HTML pages

### Key Guidelines Available
- ESPEN Guidelines on Clinical Nutrition in Hospitals
- ESPEN Guidelines on Enteral Nutrition
- ESPEN Guidelines on Parenteral Nutrition
- ESPEN Guidelines on Nutrition in Cancer Patients
- ESPEN Guidelines on Nutrition in Geriatrics

### Citations
- Cederholm, T., et al. (2017). ESPEN guidelines on definitions and terminology of clinical nutrition. Clinical Nutrition, 36(1), 49-64.
- Weimann, A., et al. (2017). ESPEN guideline: Clinical nutrition in surgery. Clinical Nutrition, 36(3), 623-650.

## 3. NICE (UK) — Nutrition Support in Adults

### Resource Status
- **URL**: https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg32
- **Status**: ✅ Active and accessible
- **Authority**: National Institute for Health and Care Excellence (NICE), UK
- **Last Verified**: 2025
- **Guideline Number**: CG32
- **Last Updated**: 2017 (with ongoing updates)

### Key Information

**Purpose**: Provides practical guidance for malnutrition assessment, nutrition support, and clinical targets in adults.

**Key Recommendations**:

1. **Malnutrition Screening**:
   - Use validated screening tools (MUST - Malnutrition Universal Screening Tool)
   - Regular screening in all care settings
   - Risk stratification (low, medium, high risk)

2. **Nutrition Support Indications**:
   - BMI <18.5 kg/m²
   - Unintentional weight loss >10% in 3-6 months
   - BMI <20 kg/m² with unintentional weight loss >5% in 3-6 months
   - Little or no nutritional intake for >5 days

3. **Energy Requirements**:
   - Use predictive equations (Schofield, Mifflin-St Jeor)
   - Apply activity factors
   - Consider clinical condition adjustments

4. **Protein Requirements**:
   - General: 0.8-1.0 g/kg body weight/day
   - Clinical conditions: 1.2-1.5 g/kg body weight/day
   - Critical illness: up to 1.5 g/kg body weight/day

5. **Monitoring**:
   - Regular weight monitoring
   - Biochemical markers (albumin, prealbumin)
   - Clinical assessment
   - Reassessment protocols

### Access Methods
- **Web Access**: Direct access via NICE website
- **PDF Downloads**: Full guideline available as PDF
- **API Access**: Available via NICE Developer API (https://developer.nice.org.uk/)
- **Format**: HTML pages, PDF documents, API (JSON/XML)

### API Information
- **Endpoint**: https://developer.nice.org.uk/
- **Authentication**: API key required (registration needed)
- **Rate Limits**: Varies by subscription level
- **Format**: JSON, XML
- **Documentation**: Available at developer portal

### Citations

## 4. KDOQI/Kidney Foundation — Nutrition in CKD (2020 Update)

### Resource Status
- **URL**: https://www.kidney.org/professionals/guidelines
- **Status**: ✅ Active and accessible
- **Authority**: National Kidney Foundation (NKF), KDOQI (Kidney Disease Outcomes Quality Initiative)
- **Last Verified**: 2025
- **Latest Update**: 2020

### Key Information

**Purpose**: Provides evidence-based guidelines for prescribing energy and protein for chronic kidney disease (CKD); essential for renal MNT.

**Key Recommendations**:

1. **Energy Requirements**:
   - CKD stages 1-5 (non-dialysis): 30-35 kcal/kg body weight/day
   - Hemodialysis: 30-35 kcal/kg body weight/day
   - Peritoneal dialysis: 30-35 kcal/kg body weight/day
   - Age >60 years: 30 kcal/kg body weight/day

2. **Protein Requirements**:
   - **CKD stages 1-2**: 0.8 g/kg body weight/day (standard protein restriction)
   - **CKD stages 3-5 (non-dialysis)**: 0.55-0.60 g/kg body weight/day (low-protein diet) with ketoacid supplementation, OR 0.8 g/kg body weight/day (moderate protein restriction)
   - **Hemodialysis**: 1.0-1.2 g/kg body weight/day
   - **Peritoneal dialysis**: 1.0-1.3 g/kg body weight/day

3. **Sodium Requirements**:
   - <2.3 g/day (<100 mmol/day)
   - Adjust based on fluid status and blood pressure

4. **Phosphorus Requirements**:
   - CKD stages 3-5: 800-1000 mg/day
   - Dialysis: 800-1000 mg/day

5. **Potassium Requirements**:
   - Individualized based on serum levels
   - Generally 2-4 g/day (50-100 mmol/day)

6. **Fluid Requirements**:
   - Individualized based on urine output, edema, and dialysis modality
   - Non-dialysis: urine output + 500-750 ml
   - Hemodialysis: typically 1-1.5 L/day
   - Peritoneal dialysis: individualized

### Access Methods
- **Web Access**: Direct access via Kidney Foundation website
- **PDF Downloads**: Guidelines available as PDF documents
- **API Access**: Not available
- **Format**: PDF documents, HTML pages

### Key Guidelines
- KDOQI Clinical Practice Guideline for Nutrition in CKD: 2020 Update
- Published in American Journal of Kidney Diseases (AJKD)

### Citations
- Ikizler, T. A., et al. (2020). KDOQI Clinical Practice Guideline for Nutrition in CKD: 2020 Update. American Journal of Kidney Diseases, 76(3), S1-S107.

## 5. EAACI — Food Allergy Guidelines

### Resource Status
- **URL**: https://www.eaaci.org/guidelines
- **Status**: ✅ Active and accessible
- **Authority**: European Academy of Allergy and Clinical Immunology (EAACI)
- **Last Verified**: 2025

### Key Information

**Purpose**: Provides evidence-based advice for intolerance/allergy management in food planning.

**Key Recommendations**:

1. **Allergen Management**:
   - Identification of major allergens (EU: 14 major allergens)
   - Cross-contamination prevention
   - Labeling requirements
   - Emergency action plans

2. **Dietary Exclusions**:
   - Evidence-based exclusion diets
   - Avoidance of unnecessary restrictions
   - Nutritional adequacy during exclusion
   - Reintroduction protocols

3. **Nutritional Considerations**:
   - Ensure nutritional adequacy during exclusion diets
   - Supplementation when necessary
   - Monitoring growth and nutritional status (especially in children)

4. **Common Allergens (EU Regulation)**:
   - Cereals containing gluten
   - Crustaceans
   - Eggs
   - Fish
   - Peanuts
   - Soybeans
   - Milk
   - Nuts
   - Celery
   - Mustard
   - Sesame
   - Sulphur dioxide/sulphites
   - Lupin
   - Molluscs

## 6. Diabetes Nutrition Therapy Consensus (ADA/EASD/National)

### Resource Status
- **URL**: https://diabetesjournals.org/care
- **Status**: ✅ Active and accessible
- **Authority**: American Diabetes Association (ADA), European Association for the Study of Diabetes (EASD)
- **Last Verified**: 2025

### Key Information

**Purpose**: Provides guidance on carbohydrate counting, macro targets, insulin-to-carbohydrate ratios, and applied diabetes nutrition therapy.

**Key Recommendations**:

1. **Macronutrient Distribution**:
   - **Carbohydrates**: Individualized, typically 45-60% of total energy
   - **Protein**: 15-20% of total energy (or 1.0-1.5 g/kg body weight/day)
   - **Fat**: 20-35% of total energy
   - Focus on quality over quantity

2. **Carbohydrate Management**:
   - Carbohydrate counting for insulin dosing
   - Glycemic index considerations
   - Fiber intake: 14 g/1000 kcal (minimum 25-30 g/day)
   - Limit added sugars: <10% of total energy

3. **Insulin-to-Carbohydrate Ratios**:
   - Individualized based on insulin sensitivity
   - Typical range: 1 unit insulin per 10-15 g carbohydrate
   - Adjust based on blood glucose patterns

4. **Meal Timing**:
   - Consistent meal timing
   - Pre-meal insulin timing
   - Postprandial glucose monitoring

5. **Special Considerations**:
   - Type 1 diabetes: intensive insulin therapy
   - Type 2 diabetes: weight management focus
   - Gestational diabetes: specific targets
   - Hypoglycemia prevention

### Access Methods
- **Web Access**: Direct access via Diabetes Care journal website
- **PDF Downloads**: Consensus reports available as PDF documents
- **API Access**: Not available (journal subscription may be required)
- **Format**: PDF documents, HTML pages

### Key Consensus Reports
- ADA/EASD Consensus Report on Type 2 Diabetes Management
- ADA Standards of Medical Care in Diabetes (annual updates)
- EASD Position Statements on Nutrition Therapy

### Citations
- Evert, A. B., et al. (2019). Nutrition Therapy for Adults With Diabetes or Prediabetes: A Consensus Report. Diabetes Care, 42(5), 731-754.
- American Diabetes Association. (2024). Standards of Medical Care in Diabetes—2024. Diabetes Care, 47(Supplement_1), S1-S300.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Macronutrient Distribution**: EFSA provides authoritative %EI ranges (Protein: 10-35%, Fat: 20-35%, Carbs: 45-65%)

2. **Protein Requirements**: Vary significantly by condition:
   - General: 0.8-1.0 g/kg/day
   - Clinical conditions: 1.2-2.0 g/kg/day
   - CKD (non-dialysis): 0.55-0.8 g/kg/day
   - Dialysis: 1.0-1.3 g/kg/day

3. **Condition-Specific Guidelines**: Each major clinical condition has specific guidelines (ESPEN for general clinical, KDOQI for CKD, ADA/EASD for diabetes, EAACI for allergies)

4. **API Access**: Only NICE provides programmatic API access; other resources are web/PDF-based

5. **Regular Updates**: Guidelines are periodically updated (KDOQI 2020, NICE 2017 with updates, ADA annual updates)
