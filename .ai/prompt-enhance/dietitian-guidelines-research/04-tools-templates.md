### Calculation Examples

**Example 1: Energy Requirement Calculation**

**Patient**: 65-year-old woman, 60 kg, 160 cm, bedridden (post-surgery)

**Step 1: Calculate BMR**
Using Mifflin-St Jeor:
```
BMR = (10 × 60) + (6.25 × 160) - (5 × 65) - 161
BMR = 600 + 1000 - 325 - 161
BMR = 1114 kcal/day
```

**Step 2: Apply PAL**
Bedridden: PAL = 1.2
```
TEE (base) = 1114 × 1.2 = 1337 kcal/day
```

**Step 3: Apply Illness Stress Factor**
Post-surgery (major): Stress factor = 1.3
```
Adjusted TEE = 1337 × 1.3 = 1738 kcal/day
```

**Step 4: Calculate Protein**
Post-surgery: 1.3 g/kg/day
```
Protein = 60 × 1.3 = 78 g/day
```

**Final Plan**:
- Energy: 1738 kcal/day
- Protein: 78 g (18% of energy)
- Fat: 30% of energy (58 g)
- Carbohydrates: 52% of energy (226 g)

- **Status**: ✅ Active and accessible
- **Authority**: World Health Organization (WHO), Food and Agriculture Organization (FAO)
- **Last Verified**: 2025

### Key Information

**Purpose**: Standard PAL (Physical Activity Level) multipliers and calculation tables for energy requirement estimation.

### Available Resources

**1. PAL Multiplier Tables**:
- Age-specific PAL values
- Activity level categories
- Gender-specific recommendations
- Population-specific values

**2. Energy Requirement Tables**:
- Pre-calculated energy requirements by age, sex, activity
- Reference values for different populations
- Adjustment factors for various conditions

**3. Calculation Guidance**:
- Step-by-step calculation methods
- Worked examples
- Best practice recommendations

- Use midpoint: 1.85
- Apply: TEE = BMR × 1.85

## 4. EFSA/FAO Index of Food-Based Dietary Guidelines (FBDGs)

### Resource Status
- **URL**: https://www.fao.org/nutrition/education/food-dietary-guidelines/en/
- **Status**: ✅ Active and accessible
- **Authority**: Food and Agriculture Organization (FAO), European Food Safety Authority (EFSA)
- **Last Verified**: 2025

### Key Information

**Purpose**: Regional and cultural food-based dietary guidelines for translating macro targets into culturally appropriate meal plans and food selections.

### Available Resources

**1. Regional FBDG Index**:
- Country-specific dietary guidelines
- Regional food patterns
- Cultural food preferences
- Traditional meal structures

**2. Food Group Classifications**:
- Standard food groups
- Serving size recommendations
- Frequency recommendations
- Portion size guidance

**3. Cultural Adaptations**:
- Mediterranean diet patterns
- Asian dietary patterns
- Latin American patterns
- African dietary patterns
- European patterns

### Key FBDG Resources

**European Countries**:
- UK: Eatwell Guide
- France: PNNS (Programme National Nutrition Santé)
- Germany: 10 Guidelines of the DGE
- Italy: Mediterranean diet guidelines
- Spain: Mediterranean diet pyramid
- Poland: Food pyramid

**Global Examples**:
- USA: MyPlate
- Canada: Canada's Food Guide
- Australia: Australian Dietary Guidelines
- Japan: Japanese Food Guide Spinning Top

### Food Group Classifications

**Standard Food Groups** (typical structure):

1. **Grains and Cereals**:
   - Whole grains preferred
   - Serving sizes: 1 slice bread, 1/2 cup cooked grains
   - Frequency: 6-11 servings/day (varies by country)

2. **Fruits**:
   - Fresh, frozen, canned (no added sugar)
   - Serving sizes: 1 medium fruit, 1/2 cup chopped
   - Frequency: 2-4 servings/day

3. **Vegetables**:
   - Variety of colors
   - Serving sizes: 1/2 cup cooked, 1 cup raw
   - Frequency: 3-5 servings/day

4. **Protein Foods**:
   - Lean meats, poultry, fish
   - Legumes, nuts, seeds
   - Serving sizes: 3-4 oz meat, 1/2 cup legumes
   - Frequency: 2-3 servings/day

5. **Dairy**:
   - Low-fat options preferred
   - Serving sizes: 1 cup milk, 1.5 oz cheese
   - Frequency: 2-3 servings/day

6. **Fats and Oils**:
   - Healthy fats preferred
   - Serving sizes: 1 tsp oil, 1 tbsp nuts
   - Frequency: Use sparingly

### Cultural Adaptations

**Mediterranean Pattern**:
- Emphasis on olive oil, fish, vegetables, fruits
- Whole grains, legumes
- Moderate wine consumption
- Limited red meat

**Asian Pattern**:
- Rice as staple
- Vegetables, soy products
- Fish, seafood
- Limited dairy

**Latin American Pattern**:
- Beans, rice, corn
- Fruits, vegetables
- Moderate meat
- Traditional preparations

### Usage in Meal Planning

**Translation Process**:
1. Calculate macro targets (from clinical guidelines)
2. Select appropriate FBDG for patient's culture/preference
3. Map macros to food groups
4. Distribute across meals
5. Ensure variety and cultural appropriateness
6. Adjust portions to meet targets

**Example**:
- Target: 2000 kcal, 20% protein, 30% fat, 50% carbs
- Using Mediterranean FBDG:
  - Breakfast: Whole grain bread, olive oil, vegetables
  - Lunch: Fish, vegetables, whole grains, olive oil
  - Dinner: Legumes, vegetables, whole grains
  - Snacks: Fruits, nuts
